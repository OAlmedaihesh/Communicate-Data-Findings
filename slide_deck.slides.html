Investigating Delayed and Cancelled Flights¶

by Omar Almedaihesh¶

Investigation Overview¶

The target of this investigation is to know what causes a flight to be delayed or cancelled, when and where it happens more often, and for how long a flights are usually delayed for.

Dataset Overview¶

The dataset consist of more than 7 million flights records for the year 2008. The dataset includes 29 features, most of which are numeric such as Departure/Arrival time, Departure/Arrival Delay. Also, some are non-numeric such as Origin, Destination, and Cancellation Code.

Distribution of Departure Delay¶

The departure delay in the dataset vary over a long range, with the majority being on the lower end. The histogram is showing a right-skewed distribution as flights delayed for less than 5 minutes is the most common.

Number of Monthly Cancelled Flights by Reason¶

In the plot we can see a relationship between winter (December to March), and flights being cancelled due to weather reasons. Overall, weather is the most common reason for flight cancellations, and it's clear that it happens more often during winter.

Average Departure Delay by Delay Probability¶

Each point in the plot represent an airport's average departure delay & probability of flight being delayed. While it's hard to say that there's a strong negative linear relationship, we can see that all airports with high average departure delay tend to have low delay probability.
